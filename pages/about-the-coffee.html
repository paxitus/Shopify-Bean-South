At Bean South, we don't compromise on what goes into your cup. Every coffee we offer meets four non-negotiable standards: fair trade, organic, shade grown, and specialty grade. These aren't marketing terms. They are commitments we make to the farmers who grow our coffee, to you, and to the planet.

Pillar 1 of 4

Fair Trade, Every Cup.

All of our coffees are Fair Trade certified. That means every farmer who grew the beans in your bag was paid a fair price for their work, operates in safe conditions, and participates in a system built for their long-term benefit.

Fair Trade represents our commitment to:

  • Long-term relationships with farming communities
  • Fair wages that sustain families and futures
  • Community development funds for schools and clinics
  • Environmental responsibility at every step
  • Transparency and democratic organization

Pillar 2 of 4

Organic Certified. Crop to Cup.

Every coffee we roast is USDA Organic certified. From the soil the plants grow in to the hands that harvest them, the entire process is done without synthetic chemicals or harmful inputs.

Organic farming is better for the farmers tending these crops without exposure to harmful pesticides. Better for their land and water. Better for the communities that live and work among these farms for generations. When you taste clean coffee, this is what clean means.

Pillar 3 of 4

Shade Grown. Ecosystem Intact.

Our coffees are grown under a canopy of shade trees, the traditional way coffee was cultivated for centuries before industrial farming cleared the forest floors.

Shade-grown farming preserves natural habitats for birds, insects, and wildlife. It maintains healthy soil and balanced ecosystems. And for the farmers and communities who live among these farms, it means a landscape that sustains life, not just crops. Great coffee and a healthy planet should not be a trade-off.

Pillar 4 of 4

Specialty Grade Only.

Specialty coffee represents the top tier of the global coffee market, scoring 80 points or above on a 100-point scale set by the Specialty Coffee Association. We roast only specialty grade.

We work with some of the most talented small-scale farmers on the planet. We take seriously the responsibility of honoring their expertise, which is why we roast every batch with the same care and attention they put into growing it. The result is a cup that stands entirely on its own.

How We Source the Coffee

No middle-man. Direct relationships. Real impact.

We are part of a unique supply chain built around fairness and direct relationships. Cooperative Coffees, our importing cooperative, buys directly from small farmer cooperatives in 14 countries around the world. No brokers. No commodity exchanges. No middle-man taking a cut from what the farmer earns.

A cooperative is a democratic enterprise owned and run by its members, who share in the profits and decisions. By working exclusively with farmer cooperatives, we ensure that the people doing the hardest work see the most direct benefit.

The Cooperative Model

We only buy from small farmer cooperatives, democratic enterprises where the farmers themselves own and operate the business and share in its profits.
